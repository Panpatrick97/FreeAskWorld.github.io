Abstract

Simulation for Human-Centric Embodied AI

FreeAskWorld is an interactive simulation framework that brings large language models into embodied navigation, enabling human-like agents that plan, communicate and move in complex urban scenes. The simulator models people, traffic, weather and daily schedules, so that navigation happens inside a dynamic social environment rather than a static maze. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

On top of this framework we extend classical Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) into a Direction Inquiry Task, where an agent can actively ask for help, interpret high-level verbal guidance, and update its plan in a closed loop. To support training and evaluation, we release the FreeAskWorld dataset with reconstructed environments, diverse avatars, six task types, 16 core object categories, 63,000+ annotated frames and over 17 hours of interaction data.

We benchmark strong VLN baselines and human participants in both open-loop and closed-loop settings. Models fine-tuned on FreeAskWorld significantly improve semantic understanding and interaction capability, but still lag behind humans on socially grounded navigation — highlighting interaction itself as an additional information modality and a key frontier for embodied AI.

Highlights

What FreeAskWorld Provides

  • LLM-driven simulation. Integrates large language models for intention modeling, instruction generation and human-like social behavior in Unity-based 3D scenes.
  • Direction Inquiry Task. Extends VLN to support asking for directions, self-assessment, information seeking and real-time plan updates under dynamic humans and vehicles.
  • Rich multimodal dataset. Reconstructed in/outdoor environments with RGB panoramas, occupancy maps, dialogs, trajectories, 2D/3D boxes, segmentation, depth and surface normals.
  • Open-loop & closed-loop benchmarks. Evaluations on BEVBert, ETPNav and their FreeAskWorld-finetuned variants, plus human baselines, under consistent metrics such as TL, SR, SPL, NE, OSR and NDI.
  • Socially grounded navigation. Shows how interaction, beyond perception alone, acts as an extra information modality for robust embodied decision making.
